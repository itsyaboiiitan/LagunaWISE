Celestial Events 2026

Date Event Visibility Notes
February 15–21, 2026 National Astronomy Week 33rd annual celebration coordinated by the Philippine Astronomical Society. Nationwide events including telescope viewings, lectures, and astrophotography workshops across major cities.
February 28, 2026 6-Planet Alignment Mercury, Venus, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, and Uranus align in the pre-dawn sky. Best viewed 45 minutes before sunrise facing east. Binoculars recommended for Uranus visibility.
May 5–6, 2026 Eta Aquariids Meteor Shower Peak viewing after midnight until dawn. Up to 30 meteors/hour under dark Philippine skies. Radiant near Aquarius constellation. Minimal moon interference.
August 12–13, 2026 Perseids Meteor Shower Peak viewing after midnight. Up to 60 meteors/hour under dark skies. Best viewed from provincial areas away from city lights. Moon sets before midnight for optimal viewing conditions.
October 20–21, 2026 Orionids Meteor Shower Peak viewing after midnight. Up to 25 meteors/hour. Radiant near Orion's club. Visible despite moderate moonlight. Best from elevated locations.
November 17–18, 2026 Leonids Meteor Shower Peak viewing after midnight. Up to 15 meteors/hour. Best viewed from dark sky locations in the Philippines. Moderate activity but historically significant.
December 13–14, 2026 Geminids Meteor Shower Peak viewing after 9 PM until dawn. Up to 120 meteors/hour under dark skies. Radiant near Castor in Gemini. Moon sets early for perfect viewing conditions.
December 24, 2026 Christmas Supermoon The final full moon of 2026 appears 14% larger and 30% brighter. Rises at sunset and visible all night. Perfect for holiday astrophotography across Philippine landscapes.
